(Phnom Penh): As part of our commitment to promoting digital literacy and responsible technology use, the Committee for Community Service Program (CCSP) proudly conducted the CyberAI Explorer initiative at the Indochina Starfish Foundation (ISF).

Throughout the program, our dedicated student volunteers introduced students to the fundamentals of cybersecurity, emphasizing the importance of protecting personal information, recognizing online threats, and practicing good cyber hygiene. In addition, participants explored the exciting world of Artificial Intelligence, learning how to write effective prompts and use AI tools responsibly, ethically, and safely.

The interactive sessions encouraged curiosity, teamwork, and hands-on learning, giving students the confidence to navigate today's digital world while understanding both the opportunities and responsibilities that come with emerging technologies.

We extend our sincere appreciation to the American University of Phnom Penh for its continuous support, the cybersecurity specialists from the Ministry of Post and Telecommunications for sharing their expertise, and the staff of the Indochina Starfish Foundation for warmly welcoming our team. Most importantly, we thank all of our student volunteers whose dedication and passion made this initiative a success.

Through CyberAI Explorer, we hope to inspire young learners to embrace technology with confidence, develop essential digital skills, and become responsible digital citizens who will shape a safer and more innovative future.
